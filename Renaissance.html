Lesson 2

What is the renaissance style?

click to hear "Renaissance"

   Renaissance architecture is a type of architecture which started in Italy around the 15th century, which is around 600 years ago. Architects learned about buildings that were made in ancient Greece and ancient Rome and started copying their style. Over the next hundred years, the Renaissance style spread all over Europe.

Why was this style so popular?

   Just before the Renaissance, there was a period called the Middle Ages that happened from the 5th to the 14th century. During the Middle Ages, there were terrible plagues and famines that made it difficult for scientific discoveries to be made. As things got better, businesses became more successful and more people had time and money for things like science and art. The wealthy began studying art and philosophy from the ancient times and trying to revive them in order to make them look more impressive. As a matter of fact, the word “Renaissance” means rebirth.


What makes this style unique?

   Just like the Roman and Greek temples of the past, many architects during this period liked to use marble in the buildings. They also liked to use a lot of columns and domes like the ones they saw in ancient ruins. They also like to use shapes like squares, circles and rectangles, and they used a lot of symmetry in their buildings. Most importantly, they used a special mathematical equation called the golden ratio to make their measurements.
